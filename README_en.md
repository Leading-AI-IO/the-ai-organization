## 📖 Overview

BCG said it plainly: **70% of the value AI creates comes from redesigning people and organizations.**

Yet nearly every company today is pouring investment into the remaining 30% — technology and data. They deploy the latest LLMs, build data pipelines, run proof-of-concept after proof-of-concept, and report "92% AI adoption rate" to their boards.

But they cannot answer the CFO's question: **"So how much money did we actually make?"**

The cause of AI adoption failure is not technology. It is the organization. Performance evaluation systems are misaligned with how value gets created in the AI era. Feedback mechanisms do not exist. High-value talent is given neither information nor authority, while their scope of responsibility expands without limit — and when results don't materialize, the failure is attributed to the individual's attitude.

**This book is not a critique of any specific organization. It is a structural autopsy.**

Drawing on world-leading cases — DeNA, Klarna, Shopify, NVIDIA, JPMorgan, Salesforce, CyberAgent, SoftBank — alongside the structural pathologies unique to Japanese organizations, this book confronts head-on the question: how should we design organizations for the AI era?
